# Module 0.0 — Introduction: What Is Gemini CLI and Why Should PMs Care?

## The Short Answer

Gemini CLI is Google's AI assistant that runs in your terminal. Unlike ChatGPT or Gemini in a browser tab, it can read and write files directly on your computer, search the web in real time, and work on your actual documents — not copies you paste in.

For product managers, this changes everything.

---

## What Makes It Different from a Browser-Based AI

| Browser AI (ChatGPT, Gemini.com) | Gemini CLI |
|----------------------------------|------------|
| You copy-paste content in | It reads your files directly |
| One question, one answer | Multi-step work across many files |
| Web search is an add-on | Google Search is built-in, always on |
| Context resets each session | GEMINI.md keeps project memory |
| Max ~32K tokens of context | 1,000,000 tokens — your entire doc library |

---

## The 1M Token Context Window — Why It Matters for PMs

One million tokens is roughly 750,000 words. That's:
- Your entire user research library from the last 2 years
- Every PRD you've ever written
- All your Notion docs, interview transcripts, and OKRs — at once

Most AI tools make you choose what to include. Gemini CLI can hold all of it at once and reason across everything simultaneously.

**Example:** "Read all 47 customer interviews from Q1 and tell me the top 3 activation blockers, how they differ by company size, and which persona mentions them most."

That's a task that would take a PM analyst a week. Gemini CLI can do it in minutes.

---

## What You Can Do With It (PM Edition)

### Research & Synthesis
- Synthesize hundreds of user interviews, feedback entries, and support tickets
- Research competitors using live web data (not 6-month-old training data)
- Summarize long strategy docs with specific questions in mind

### Documents & Writing
- Draft PRDs from scratch with your company context baked in
- Get PRDs reviewed from the perspective of engineering, sales, and users simultaneously
- Rewrite docs for different audiences (exec summary vs. engineering spec)

### Analysis
- Parse CSV exports from Mixpanel, Amplitude, or your data warehouse
- Surface patterns in qualitative data
- Identify contradictions in your roadmap or OKRs

### Strategy
- Run competitive analysis using real-time web research
- Pressure-test your positioning with AI playing devil's advocate
- Model different prioritization frameworks against your backlog

### Building
- Create simple internal tools (dashboards, templates, scripts) without writing code
- Automate repetitive PM tasks (weekly update drafts, ticket formatting)

---

## What You Won't Be Doing

- Writing code by hand
- Learning terminal commands
- Memorizing syntax

You'll work the same way you work with a smart colleague: describe what you want, provide the context, and ask for the output. The only new skill is knowing what to ask.
