# Gemini CLI for Product Managers

**A free, hands-on course. No coding experience required.**

Learn to use Gemini CLI — Google's free AI tool — to write better PRDs, analyze product data, research competitors, and build internal PM tools. All inside your terminal. All for free.

[Browse the Lessons](#modules){ .md-button }

---

## Why Gemini CLI?

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   **Free with your Google account**

    No subscription needed. If you use Gmail or Google Workspace, you already have access. Same account your company approved.

-   **1M token context window**

    Load your entire research library — every interview transcript, PRD, and OKR doc — and ask questions across all of it at once.

-   **Google Search built in**

    Real-time competitive research, market benchmarks, and company news. Not training data from 6 months ago.

-   **No coding required**

    You work the same way you work with a smart colleague. Describe what you want. Get useful output. Iterate.

</div>

---

## What You'll Be Able to Do

After completing this course, you will:

- **Synthesize research** — process 50 user interviews in minutes, not days
- **Write better PRDs** — stress-test your spec from the perspective of engineering, sales, and users before it leaves your laptop
- **Analyze product data** — find funnel drop-offs and surface insights without SQL or a data analyst queue
- **Run competitive intelligence** — live web research turned into a positioning strategy and sales battlecard
- **Build PM tools** — create working internal tools (dashboards, generators, trackers) without writing code

## The Company You'll Work With

Throughout the course, you're the **Senior PM for Growth & Onboarding at Stride** — a product management SaaS for distributed teams, Series A, 6,500 users.

You've inherited a problem: activation is at 23% (industry benchmark: 40–50%). You have 30 days to fix it. Gemini CLI is your tool.

Every exercise uses real-looking company data — metrics, user feedback, personas, a product roadmap. The company is fictional. The work is not.
