# Module 2.3 — Competitive Strategy: Live Research to Positioning

## The Competitive Intelligence Problem

Competitive analysis in most PM teams is either:
- **Stale:** A deck that was accurate 8 months ago and hasn't been updated
- **Shallow:** Feature comparison tables with no strategic insight
- **Slow:** Weeks of analyst time to produce a half-useful report

Gemini CLI with Google Search grounding changes all three.

---

## Stride's Competitive Landscape

Stride's main competitors for product roadmap and PM workflow tools:

| Competitor | Primary Strength | Primary Weakness (as we see it) |
|------------|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| Productboard | Feedback integration, enterprise | Expensive, complex, engineering-unfriendly |
| Linear | Speed, engineering-led | PM-lite, weak roadmapping, no stakeholder layer |
| Notion | Flexibility, familiarity | No structure, everything is a blank page |
| Craft.io | Roadmap visualization | Niche, weak integrations |
| Jira (Atlassian) | Ecosystem, enterprise | PM experience is secondary to engineering |

## The Competitive Research Workflow

### Step 1: Current State of Each Competitor
```
Use Google Search to research Productboard's product updates in the last 6 months.
What new features have they shipped? What are PMs saying about them on LinkedIn, Reddit, or review sites?
Summarize in 3 bullets per competitor. Cover: Productboard, Linear, and Notion.
```

### Step 2: Identify Competitive Moves
```
Based on what you found: 
- Is any competitor moving into our core territory (onboarding, team activation)?
- Is any competitor showing weakness we could exploit?
- What's the most significant market shift in the last 6 months?
```

### Step 3: Positioning Analysis
```
@company-context/overview.md @company-context/personas.md

Given Stride's positioning (distributed team PM platform) and our three personas:
1. Where do we genuinely win today?
2. Where are we losing deals and why?
3. What's the one positioning bet we should make in the next 6 months?

Search for recent competitor pricing and compare to ours ($23/seat/month, minimum 3 seats).
```

### Step 4: Battlecard for Sales
```
Write a 1-page competitive battlecard for Stride vs. Productboard.
Format:
- When you win: 3 situations where we beat Productboard
- When they win: 3 situations where Productboard beats us (be honest)
- Key differentiators: 3 things only we do
- Trap questions: 3 questions Productboard sales reps ask that we should anticipate
- Our response: suggested answer for each

Audience: Leila Torres (our Head of Sales), to use in enterprise deals.
```

### Step 5: Strategic Implication for Roadmap
```
@company-context/roadmap.md

Given this competitive landscape, review our Q2–Q3 roadmap.
Are we investing in the right areas to win? 
What's one thing we should accelerate, one thing we should de-prioritize, and one gap we're not addressing?
```

### Step 6: Save the Output
```
Save the full competitive analysis to outputs/competitive-analysis-april-2026.md
Include: competitor summaries, positioning analysis, battlecard, and roadmap recommendations.
```

## Staying Current: The Ongoing Competitive Ritual

One of the most valuable things you can do weekly (takes 10 minutes with Gemini):

```
Search for news about [competitor] in the last 7 days.
Has anything changed in their product, pricing, funding, or leadership?
Does this change anything about how we should position against them?
```

Set this up as a weekly habit. You'll always know more about your competitive landscape than you have time to know without AI.
