# Stride — User Personas

These are the three primary user types you'll reference throughout the course.
When writing PRDs, features, or strategies — always pressure-test against all three.

---

## Persona 1: Aisha — The Enterprise PM Lead

**Role:** Director of Product, enterprise SaaS company (500 employees)
**Location:** New York (team is across 4 time zones)
**Tools she uses today:** Jira, Confluence, Google Slides, Notion, Miro

### Goals
- Keep 3 product squads aligned without constant status meetings
- Give exec team visibility into roadmap without giving them edit access
- Document decisions so new hires can get context without bugging senior PMs

### Frustrations
- Jira is where tickets go to die — nobody reads comments or docs
- Every quarter she rebuilds the same roadmap deck from scratch
- "I spend 40% of my time on alignment work, not actual product thinking"

### How She Uses Stride
- Roadmap Studio for quarterly planning (loves dependency view)
- Alignment Hub for weekly exec updates (saves 3 hours/week)
- Decision Log to onboard new PMs
- Biggest blocker: getting engineers to update ticket status

### Quote
*"If I could get my engineers to update 5 fields in Stride, I could cancel 3 recurring meetings."*

---

## Persona 2: Dev — The Startup PM/Founder

**Role:** Head of Product (also acts as PM) at a 12-person B2B startup
**Location:** Remote (SF timezone)
**Tools he uses today:** Linear, Notion, Loom, Slack, Google Docs

### Goals
- Ship fast and stay flexible — roadmaps change weekly
- Keep the engineering team unblocked with clear specs
- Track product decisions so he stops re-litigating the same debates

### Frustrations
- Too many tools — context scattered across Notion, Linear, Slack threads
- Stakeholder updates are informal — CEO just pings him on Slack
- "I don't have time to maintain a Notion doc AND a Jira board AND a roadmap deck"

### How He Uses Stride
- Sprint Bridge to connect product priorities to Linear tickets
- Decision Log as his "thinking out loud" space (quick, unstructured)
- Would use Alignment Hub if it could post to Slack automatically
- Biggest frustration: Stride feels too structured for his workflow speed

### Quote
*"I need something that works the way I think, not the way a PM textbook says I should think."*

---

## Persona 3: Marcus — The Engineering Manager

**Role:** EM at a 60-person mid-stage SaaS company
**Location:** Berlin (team is 70% remote)
**Tools he uses today:** Jira, GitHub, Confluence, Linear

### Goals
- Understand *why* each ticket exists, not just what to build
- Reduce back-and-forth with PM during sprint planning
- Track which decisions led to which outcomes (for post-mortems)

### Frustrations
- PRDs are either too vague ("make it better") or too prescriptive ("pixel-perfect this Figma")
- Scope changes mid-sprint without explanation or context
- "I open Confluence maybe twice a year. If it's not in Jira or Slack, it doesn't exist."

### How He Uses Stride
- Sprint Bridge — his primary touchpoint with product decisions
- Reads Decision Log before starting major features
- Doesn't use Roadmap Studio (that's the PM's job)
- Loves when PMs link Stride decisions directly from Jira tickets

### Quote
*"I don't need to be in every product meeting. I just need to understand the 'why' before we start building."*

---

## Using Personas in Your Work

When writing a PRD, run this quick check:
- Would Aisha feel confident presenting this roadmap to her exec team?
- Would Dev be able to ship against this spec without a meeting?
- Would Marcus understand why this is worth building before writing a single line of code?

If all three say yes — you're in good shape.
